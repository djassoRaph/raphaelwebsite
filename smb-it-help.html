The problem with IT support designed for big companies

You call the agency. You get a ticket number. Someone who doesn't know your setup asks you to restart your router. You explain the situation again. They escalate it. You wait. Your afternoon is gone.

IT support built for enterprises assumes hundreds of employees, standardised equipment, and someone on your side who speaks IT. None of that is true for a 10-person company where two people share one printer and the server is a NAS box under someone's desk.

Small company IT is a different problem. It needs a different solution. Not a helpdesk. Not a contract. One senior engineer who knows your setup and answers when you call.

What IT help for small companies actually looks like

One person who knows your setup

Not a ticket queue. Not a rotating junior. The same senior engineer, every time who already knows your systems, your team, and your history of weird problems.

Plain English, always

You don't need to know what a DNS record is or why your SSL certificate matters. You just need to know if your website is working. I handle the rest.

Remote-first no callout fees

Most problems are solved remotely in under an hour. No travel time, no callout charges, no "we'll send someone next Tuesday." Just a fix and an explanation.

Everything a small team needs

Email issues, slow computers, new staff setup, backup checks, website problems, software updates, security the full picture, handled by one person.

Problems caught before they hurt

Failing backups, expiring certificates, outdated systems these rarely announce themselves until it's too late. I check them before they become your emergency.

Fixed monthly cost, no surprises

You know what IT costs every month. No invoices for "time spent researching your problem." No surprises. That's how IT support for small businesses should work.

Who this is for

This works best for companies with 5 to 40 employees who have no dedicated IT person and no plans to hire one. You might be a professional services firm, a retail business, a small manufacturer, a creative agency, or anything in between.

Common questions

What IT help does a small company actually need?

Most small companies need someone available when things break, someone to catch problems before they become emergencies, and someone who can handle new employee setup and routine maintenance. You don't need a department; you need a relationship with one experienced person.

Why shouldn't small businesses use an IT agency?

Agencies are built for scale, not for small businesses. You pay for account managers, junior staff who rotate, and ticket queues. The person who helped you last month won't be the person who helps you this month. A senior engineer on a monthly plan gives you consistency, expertise, and a direct line for less money.

How much does IT help for a small company cost?

Monthly support for small companies starts at €75/month for teams of 1–5 people, and goes up to €400+/month for companies with more complex infrastructure. There are no hourly surprises, no callout fees, and no minimum contract term. See full pricing →

Can I get IT support without signing a long contract?

Yes. Plans are month-to-month; there is no long-term commitment required. If it doesn't work for you, you stop. The goal is to earn your business every month, not to lock you in.

What's the difference between IT help for small companies versus enterprise IT support?

Enterprise IT support is built for hundreds of employees, complex infrastructure, and compliance-heavy environments. Small company IT help is built for reality: a team that shares two printers, uses three different cloud tools, and needs someone who can fix the problem and explain it in plain English.
