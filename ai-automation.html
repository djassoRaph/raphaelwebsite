AI Business Automation

You spend hours every week on things a machine could handle in seconds. Sending invoices. Chasing payments. Copying data between tools. Writing the same email for the fifteenth time.

I build automation systems that do that work for you — quietly, reliably, without you having to learn a single line of code. You control everything from your phone, via Telegram. The system runs 24/7, even when you're asleep.

What does "automation" actually mean?

In plain terms: instead of you doing a repetitive task, a program does it automatically when certain conditions are met.

Example: Automatic invoicing

You finish a job. You send a Telegram message: "Invoice client Dupont, plumbing repair, €320." The system creates the invoice, fills in the client's details, sends it by email, and logs it in your spreadsheet. You did nothing except send one message.

Example: Payment reminders

An invoice hasn't been paid after 14 days. The system automatically sends a polite reminder to the client — with the right invoice attached — without you having to remember or do anything. You get notified when it's done.

Example: Email triage

New emails arrive in your inbox. The system reads them, categorises them (client inquiry, invoice, spam, urgent), and sends you a daily summary via Telegram. You open your inbox once a day and only see what matters.

Example: Parts & stock lookup

You're on a job and need to check if a part is in stock. You photograph the barcode and send it to the bot. It checks your stock list and replies in seconds. No spreadsheet to open, no phone call to make.

Example: Daily business reports

Every morning at 8am, the system sends you a summary: invoices outstanding, jobs scheduled today, revenue this week vs last week. You start your day informed, not searching for information.

Example: Voice-to-invoice

You finish a job on-site and speak into your phone: "Job done at rue de la Paix, replaced water pump, 3 hours labour, parts €140." The system transcribes it, creates the invoice, and files it — before you've even left the car park.

How it works — the short version

You don't need to understand the technical details to benefit from this. But here's roughly what's under the hood.

n8n — the workflow engine

n8n is the brain of the system. It connects your tools together and defines the rules: "When this happens → do that." It can talk to email, Google Sheets, your accounting tool, Telegram, WhatsApp, your website, almost anything. Think of it as the plumbing that makes everything flow together.

Self-hosted on your server — your data never passes through a third-party service.

Claude / AI — the intelligence

When a task requires understanding language — reading an email, categorising a request, generating text, interpreting a voice message — the system uses an AI model (Claude, by Anthropic). This is what lets the bot understand "invoice Dupont for the pump job" rather than requiring you to fill in a form.

The AI only sees what you send it. It doesn't have access to your entire business.

Telegram — your control panel

Telegram is how you talk to your automation system. Send a message, get a reply. Ask it to create an invoice, check stock, pull a report. It feels like texting a very competent assistant. No new app to learn — you already have Telegram on your phone.

The hardware — a small box that runs it all

The system runs on a Raspberry Pi, a Mac Mini, or a cheap VPS (a virtual server you rent for €5/month). It's on 24/7, uses barely any power, and sits quietly in the corner doing its job. Your data stays in your building, on your machine.

API calls — connecting your existing tools

Your accounting software, your booking system, your online shop, your bank — most modern tools have an API: a way for other software to talk to them automatically. The automation system uses these connections to pull data in and push data out, so nothing needs to be entered twice.
