What actually happens when no one is in charge of IT

Someone's laptop runs slow. The printer stops working. An email bounces and nobody knows why. The website went down at some point last week and you only found out because a client mentioned it.

These problems don't disappear. The slow laptop becomes the bottleneck in a client presentation. The bounced email was a proposal that never arrived. The website issue lost you a lead you'll never know about.

Having no IT department doesn't mean you have no IT problems. It means nobody is dealing with them. You are caught in between everything else you're trying to do.

Your options and what they actually cost

Expensive

Hire an IT agency

Agencies work fine for large companies with complex needs. For a 10-person business, you're paying for account managers, junior staff rotation, and a ticket queue not a relationship.

Typically €300–€800/month, often with long-term contracts.

Risky

Hire someone full-time

A junior IT hire costs €35,000–€55,000 per year before benefits. For most small businesses, there isn't enough work to justify that; and a junior hire won't have seen the situations that matter most.

€2,900–€4,600/month fully loaded.

Costly

Call someone when things break

Break-fix pricing sounds flexible until you're paying a €150 callout fee to fix something that took 20 minutes. And you still don't have anyone looking out for problems before they become emergencies.

€100–€200 per incident; unlimited surprises.

Recommended

Senior engineer on a monthly plan

One experienced person who knows your setup, available when you need them. Remote-first, fixed monthly cost, no agency markup. You get more expertise for less money than any other option.

From €75/month. See pricing →

Common questions

What should a small business do if they have no IT department?

The most practical solution for most small businesses is to have a senior IT engineer available on a monthly basis; someone who handles problems when they arise and keeps an eye on things between calls. This costs far less than hiring even a junior IT employee, and you get more experience, not less.

Can a business with 5 employees really afford IT support?

Yes. Monthly plans for small teams start at €75/month; less than most businesses spend on software subscriptions they've forgotten about. The question isn't whether you can afford it; it's whether you can afford not having it when something breaks.

Do I need to hire someone full-time to get proper IT support?

Not if you have fewer than 50 employees. A full-time IT hire costs €35,000–€55,000 per year before benefits. Most small businesses need someone available, not someone employed full-time. A senior engineer on a monthly plan gives you the expertise without the overhead.

What problems can remote IT support handle without being on-site?

The vast majority of small business IT problems are solvable remotely: email issues, software problems, slow computers, backup failures, website downtime, user account issues, security questions, printer drivers, and more. On-site visits are rarely necessary.

Is it safe to let someone access my business systems remotely?

Yes, when done properly. Remote access tools require your explicit permission each time; you see everything happening on screen. This is standard practice for IT support worldwide.
